Discovery, in its current state, uses hardly 10% of the computational power that the student laptops provide, and while underusing our technology we make sure to cash in on back to school paper discounts; all too often resorting to paper based handouts when we could use technology. Just last year during beta club elections we turned in folded slips of paper instead of submitting a digital vote, months beforehand, we circled names to vote for our senators, when a digital collection system would have been faster and more secure. Every year, we choose our families, schedules, and enrichments through paper. Why? Every student here has a laptop, phone, or some sort of internet access. We are all technology literate, we know the language of the internet and we understand what it can be used for, so why have we neglected the use of technology to automate these mundane tasks? Even if we did use Google Forms as a means of gathering and storing data, that’s still thinking small. We can do more than fill-in-the-blank forms, we can create a school that is really based on technology. With old laptop screens, we can fill the halls with monitors to share and celebrate events around the school and the world. With a few lines of code, we can create a universal Math chat for Discovery students with a competitive ranking system (see here). With simple data analysis and attention to detail we can even create a mutually beneficial tutoring system for teachers to use. But we haven’t. Instead, we use technology to, at best, do paper assignments online, and at worst, supplement printed assignments.

                What the future of Discovery looks like to me, is one where we don’t use technology as a gimmick, rather, as something that is intertwined with everything we do here. Allowing us to truly build a school that is 21-century ready.

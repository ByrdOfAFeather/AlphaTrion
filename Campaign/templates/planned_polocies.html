Discovery, in its current state, uses hardly 10% of the computational power that the student laptops provide. We all too often resort to paper based handouts when we could use technology. Just last year during beta club elections we turned in folded slips of paper instead of submitting a digital vote. Two years ago, we circled names to vote for our senators, when a digital collection system would have been faster and more secure. Every year, we choose our families, schedules, and enrichments through paper. Why? Every student here has a laptop, phone, or some sort of internet access. We are all technology literate, we know the language of the internet and we understand what it can be used for, so why have we neglected the use of technology? I say that it’s up to the senate to move this school towards its promise of a technology fueled education, that instead of using paper based information collection, we use what the school gives us.  All across our halls we line them with paper announcements, why? TV screens are expensive, to be sure, but we all have broken a laptop or two, each with screens still as usable as before. Why haven’t we lined the halls with inexpensive and easy-to-setup screens in the school? They can serve the purpose of announcements, celebration of student achievement, and connection of the outside community to Discovery.

                What the future of Discovery looks like to me, is one where we don’t use technology as a gimmick, rather, as something that is intertwined with everything we do here. Allowing us to truly build a school that is 21-century ready.
